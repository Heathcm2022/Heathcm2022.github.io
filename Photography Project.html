I took this picture at a Navigators retreat in Carlilse. I layed back in my hammock to get the verticle point-of-view. The only effect I used was the automatic glare reducer. There was no zoom, and it was taken as a still photo (as opposed to a live photo like I phones can take).

For this photo, I raised the brightness and the contrast to keep the distinction in the layers of the tree branches, but also make it pop more. I also adjusted the sharpness because when you used the contrast tool it makes it blury or pizelated sometimes, so adjusting the sharpness minorly can counteract that. I also used the brightening brush just in the middle there where the main source of light is.

This picture was taken just before one of my bands gigs while we were setting up. I used my I phone 12s .5 camera so I could capture the set up of everything. I also used the auto adjust brightness setting so it wouldn’t glare off the cymbals of the guitar.

This picture was taken in Hilton Head with my girlfriends I phone 13, using the standard camera. The phone was held so that it was just a few inches off the ground so we could catch the sun light and the reflection on the waves. The automatic brightness/glare adjuster was used just on the sun itself, with no other work done.

I started out with adjusting the contrast to make the colors fade together more, to take away the darkness on the horizon line. I then use the brightening brush on the horison line and sky to bring out the blue and orange while keeping it calm. I very minorly adjusted the sharpnes using the unsharpen mask, just to make the bubbles more cutout and defined.



For this photo, I used the sharpen tool to make the edges and depth of the room to pop. I cropped out the sound module on the right side. I also used the brighten tool on the cymbals and on the guitarist, since I used the glare reduction tool on my phone, then I could make them pop in post without risking glare. I adjusted the shadows there in the background as well as the coloring to make it more yellow and slightly brighter so that it contrasted color-wise, but that it wasn’t dark and looked creepy.
